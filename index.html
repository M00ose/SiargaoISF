Festival Activities

Come together for Siargao International Surfing Festival’s 26th year.

October 16, 2022

ISLAND MERCATO

Be greeted by mini food trucks and stalls from Siargao’s most famous bars and restaurants. You’re in for a culinary adventure on this one.

A portion of the profits will be donated to local businesses that were affected by the pandemic and Typhoon Odette. #BangonSiargao

October 17, 2022

SIARGAO ART FEST

Support local artisans in a flea market-styled bazaar. From handcrafted accessories to paintings and sculptures, you can see and support the Siargao art scene all in one place.

A portion of the profits will be donated to local businesses that were affected by the pandemic and Typhoon Odette. #BangonSiargao

October 18, 2022

SIARGAO SOUND SESSIONS

General Luna invites you to experience different sounds in different stages. To make it even better, Tanduay will serve free drinks, all in the name of companionship.

October 19, 2022

SIARGAO YESTERDAY, TODAY, AND TOMORROW

To recognize the communal efforts of the Siargao-nons, LGUs, Filipinos, and the people of the world in helping the island recover, we’ll feature recordings of Siargao before and after its most recent challenges.

October 20, 2022

CAMP SIARGAO

Camping and camaraderie go hand-in-hand together. On the second to the last night of the 26th Siargao International Surfing Festival, we/’ll host a huddle around a campfire. The best part? We’ll serve marshmallows and s’mores kits plus some drinks.

October 21, 2022

THE LAST FULL SHOW: A BENEFIT CONCERT

Headlined by some of the best acts in the local music scene and Siargao’s finest musicians, a concert by the beach will take place on the last day of the week-long celebration.

A portion of the profits will be donated to local businesses that were affected by the pandemic and Typhoon Odette. #BangonSiargao

About Us

The Siargao International Surfing Festival is one of the biggest surfing events in the Philippines where the whole Siargao community makes an effort to bring back the island’s energy through surfing, music, and food.

The recent years have been challenging for the island, its people, and its patrons brought about by the COVID-19 pandemic and Typhoon Odette. However, the strong sense of community didn’t let the spirits of the Surigaonons down. With the help of LGUs and the private sector, the island is beginning to stand tall as it once was.

After two years, the Siargao International Surfing Festival is finally back, bigger and better. This year’s celebration is in honor of all the Surigaonons especially the ones who stood tall for their families, friends, and the community despite all the difficulties.
